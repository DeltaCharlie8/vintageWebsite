A common misconception when a beginner starts embriodery is looking at all of the options and assuming they need them all, in actuality embriodery is inexpensive and easy you learn the fundamentals. Stripping embriodery supplies to simple necessity would be good to do for a beginner, until they grasp everything; fabrics of your choice, embriodery thread (often referred to as floss), a veriety of needles, water-soluble pen and a pair of scissors. This is the most common requirements and really all you need to create beautiful embriodery. After buying the necessities and having played around alittle with some ideas, you will need to harness the most frequently used stitches, the main stitches used are; Running stitch, chain stitch, back stitch, satin stitch, french knot and lastly lazy daisy.

Threads

There are a variety of threads you can use when doing embroidery, the most common being cotton which comes in 6 strands. This can come in more than 500 different colours from single colours to threads with blended colours. These can be found in any craft shop with the most common brands being Anchor and DMC. Other popular threads include Metallic and Silk which are great for a strong and shiny finish to your work! They go particularly well with a contrasting fabric such as linen or evenweave.
